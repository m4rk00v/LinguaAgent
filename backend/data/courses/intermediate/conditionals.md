# Conditionals

## First Conditional (real/possible future)
- Structure: If + present simple, will + infinitive
- "If it rains, I will stay home."
- "If you study hard, you will pass the exam."

## Second Conditional (unreal/hypothetical present)
- Structure: If + past simple, would + infinitive
- "If I had more money, I would travel the world."
- "If she spoke English, she would get the job."

## Third Conditional (unreal past)
- Structure: If + past perfect, would have + past participle
- "If I had studied, I would have passed."
- "If they had left earlier, they would have arrived on time."

## Common Mistakes
- Wrong: "If I will see him, I tell him" → Correct: "If I see him, I will tell him"
- Wrong: "If I would have money, I would buy it" → Correct: "If I had money, I would buy it"
