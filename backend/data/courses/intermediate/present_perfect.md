# Present Perfect

## Structure
- Subject + have/has + past participle
- Negative: Subject + haven't/hasn't + past participle
- Question: Have/Has + subject + past participle?

## When to Use
- Experiences: "I have visited London twice."
- Changes: "She has become more confident."
- Unfinished actions: "I have worked here for 5 years."
- Recent actions: "He has just left."

## Key Words
- Ever, never, already, yet, just, since, for

## Present Perfect vs Simple Past
- "I have been to Paris." (experience, time not important)
- "I went to Paris last year." (specific time in the past)

## Common Mistakes
- Wrong: "I have went to Paris" → Correct: "I have gone to Paris"
- Wrong: "She has ate lunch" → Correct: "She has eaten lunch"
- Wrong: "I have visited London yesterday" → Correct: "I visited London yesterday"
