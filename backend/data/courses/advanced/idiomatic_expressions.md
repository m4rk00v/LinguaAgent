# Idiomatic Expressions

## Work & Business
- "Break the ice": Start a conversation in a social situation.
- "Think outside the box": Be creative, find unconventional solutions.
- "Get the ball rolling": Start a process or activity.
- "On the same page": In agreement, sharing the same understanding.
- "Cut corners": Do something in the easiest or cheapest way.

## Everyday Life
- "Hit the nail on the head": Be exactly right about something.
- "A piece of cake": Something very easy.
- "Under the weather": Feeling sick or unwell.
- "Break a leg": Good luck (especially before a performance).
- "Cost an arm and a leg": Very expensive.

## Usage Tips
- Idioms are informal — avoid them in academic writing.
- Context matters — the same idiom can feel different in different situations.
- Learn them in context, not as isolated phrases.
